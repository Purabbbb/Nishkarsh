The Problem

The Challenge: The "Accessibility Barrier" in Research

  • 01

    Information Overload

    Over 2.5 million scientific papers are published annually. Keeping up with the latest research is nearly impossible.

  • 02

    The Complexity Wall

    Research papers are written by experts for experts. They use dense jargon and complex math that creates a massive barrier for students and beginners.

  • 03

    Static & One-Way

    A PDF is a "dead" format. You cannot ask it to explain a specific equation or clarify a doubt. It is a monologue, not a dialogue.

  • 04

    The Language Gap

    For non-native English speakers, understanding high-level academic vocabulary is a double struggle.

Our Solution

An Intelligent NLP Research Assistant

01

Core Feature

A Fine‑Tuned Summarization Model that doesn't just shorten text, but rewrites it in "Layman's Terms" OR Abstractive Summary (Simulated Teacher Explanation) for each findings, methodology, conclusion and limitation.

02

Interactive Q&A (RAG)

A Retrieval-Augmented Generation (RAG) chatbot that allows users to ask specific questions like "What dataset did they use?" or "Explain the math in equation 3".

03

Accessibility Features

Multilingual Support: Instant translation of summaries into native languages (e.g., Hindi).

Voice Output: Audio walkthroughs of the summary for auditory learners using Text to Speech Models.
